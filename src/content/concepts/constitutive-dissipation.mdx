## The one-sentence version

**Persistence is priced, and the price is the regime.** A self-regenerating closure is not a substance that happens to dissipate energy; it is a pattern of dissipation that, by occurring, keeps regenerating its own conditions. Stop the dissipation and the regime is not just damaged. It is gone.

## Where the word comes from

The seed is in Schrödinger's *What Is Life?* (1944), where organisms are described as feeding on negative entropy — sustaining an unlikely order by exporting disorder. Prigogine's *dissipative structures* (Brussels school, 1960s onwards) generalised the idea: certain systems far from equilibrium organise themselves precisely because energy is flowing through them. The closure-of-constraints framework of Montévil and Mossio (2015) tightens it further: organisms are networks of constraints whose maintenance and the processes they constrain mutually depend on one another.

[Ontogony](/concepts/ontogony) takes the next step. The dissipation is not merely *enabling* of identity; it is *constitutive* of it. There is no underlying thing that has the dissipation as one of its properties. The dissipation is the thing.

## Why it matters

The thesis turns identity into something measurable.

**It distinguishes closure from mere flow.** A waterfall dissipates energy. A candle flame dissipates energy. Neither regenerates the conditions of its own dissipation. A cell does. Constitutive dissipation is the criterion that lets you tell those cases apart without resorting to vocabulary about "life" or "purpose": is the dissipation regenerating the boundary conditions of its own continuation, or not? If yes, the dissipation is constitutive; if no, it is just throughput.

**It makes the cessation signature legible.** When a regime's dissipation stops, what dissolves first reveals what was being maintained at the highest cost. The order of collapse is not arbitrary; it inverts the order of energetic commitment. This is empirical, repeatable, and indexed to the regime's own conditions of viability rather than to an outside observer's taxonomy.

**It compounds up the [recursive stack](/concepts/recursive-stack).** Higher layers in the stack are paid for partly out of the constitutive dissipations of the layers beneath them. Cognitive maintenance rents from biological maintenance, which rents from physical-chemical gradients. The framework is therefore not just about cells; it is about any layer at which a closure regenerates itself, and about how those layers compose.

The point can be put as a slogan: **a regime is not what it is; it is what it costs.**

## What it is not

- Not **energy throughput** in general. Anything dissipates energy. Constitutive dissipation names the specific case in which the dissipation is the regeneration of the conditions for its own continuation.
- Not **entropy production** in general. The second law guarantees entropy production for any irreversible process; constitutive dissipation requires, additionally, that the process closes back on itself in a way that produces a regime with its own conditions of failure.
- Not **a metaphor**. Burn rates are SI-measurable. Cessation signatures are observable. The framework's central claim is that identity has a unit, and the unit is the watt.
- Not **restricted to biology**. Any regime that regenerates its own boundary conditions through dissipation qualifies — chemical, biological, cognitive, or institutional. The bill takes different forms at different layers; the structure is the same.
