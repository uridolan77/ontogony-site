## The one-sentence version

**Transduction is the irreversible event by which a metastable field resolves into a distinct regime — a closure that did not exist before and cannot be undone without further transductive work.** It is ontogony's core operator.

## Where the term comes from

Simondon introduced transduction to describe how pre-individual potential crystallises into individuated actuality without remainder. Ontogony extends the term beyond individuation to any level-generating resolution: physical phase transitions, organic cell-fate decisions, cognitive reframings, and institutional bifurcations are all transductions at their respective registers.

## Why it matters

Every level of reality — every regime with its own burn rate, norm-set, and cessation signature — arrived through a transduction. To understand how levels are real and not merely epistemic, you must understand how fields resolve; and that resolution is always irreversible, always costly, and always productive of novelty that could not have been read off the prior state.
