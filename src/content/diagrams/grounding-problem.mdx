## The one-sentence version

**The hard problem of grounding is the problem of how anything physical can mean anything at all.** The gap between substrate and semantics persists across every philosophical tradition, from Descartes' pineal gland to contemporary neuroscience.

## Where the term comes from

The term "hard problem" invokes David Chalmers' framing but applies it to a deeper question: not consciousness itself, but the precondition for consciousness—the binding between physical organization and semantic content. This is the classical binding problem of philosophy of mind.

## Why it matters

Every theory of life, mind, or intelligence must answer: how does the physical system acquire the capacity to *mean* something beyond itself? This is the threshold at which ontogony begins.
