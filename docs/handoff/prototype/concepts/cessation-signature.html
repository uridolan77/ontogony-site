CONCEPT · ENTRY 014 · R2 BIOLOGICAL

Cessation signature

The regime-specific sequence of collapses that maps, by what dissolves and in what order, exactly what was being maintained.

Register
R2  biological — SI-measurable at the cellular scale; operationalised by metabolic proxies at the neural; programmatic at the institutional.
Definition
The ordered breakdown trajectory of a closure when its throughput is severed. Because the most energetically expensive achievements unravel first, the trajectory is the closure's organisational hierarchy, read backward.
Extended

Sever the worm's nutrient supply and its constraint-architecture comes apart in a specific order. Membrane potentials flicker as ATP-dependent ion pumps lose their substrate. Compartments dissolve. The boundary between organism and environment softens. What persists is a smear of organic molecules equilibrating with the surrounding medium.

The signature is discovered, not presupposed: it is read off the system's own dynamics, indexed to its own conditions of viability. The vocabulary it produces is the closure's own — not the observer's projection.

What it is not
  • not a death curve, nor a generic decay
  • not entropy production in general — entropy fields don't have an order
  • not a metaphor borrowed from medicine
  • not equivalent to system failure in engineering — engineered systems fail in their designer's vocabulary, closures fail in their own
Genealogy
Diagnostic complement to burn rate. Operationalises the categorical cut Deacon identified between morphodynamic and teleodynamic regimes. Adjacent to Montévil & Mossio's closure of constraints, but adds the failure trajectory their framework lacks.
